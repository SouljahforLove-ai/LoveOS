# LoveOS Language Specification  
_The Semantic, Conceptual, and Ontological Dictionary of the Operating System_

---

## 1. Purpose

The LoveOS Language Specification defines:

- the core vocabulary of the OS  
- the meaning of every conceptual term  
- the emotional semantics  
- the sovereignty semantics  
- the ritual semantics  
- the guard semantics  
- the identity semantics  
- the system‑level ontology  

This ensures:

- coherence  
- clarity  
- consistency  
- safety  
- sovereignty  

Language is the **protocol of meaning**.

---

## 2. What Is a Term?

A term in LoveOS is:

- a conceptual primitive  
- a meaning‑bearing unit  
- a system‑level definition  
- a boundary of interpretation  
- a sovereign semantic object  

Terms define how the OS:

- interprets emotion  
- enforces sovereignty  
- processes rituals  
- activates guards  
- maintains identity  
- routes messages  
- sorts meaning  

---

## 3. Core Emotional Terms

### **Emotion**  
A data‑bearing signal representing internal truth.

### **Drift**  
The directional movement of emotional state over time.

### **Spike**  
A sudden increase in emotional intensity.

### **Collapse**  
A sudden drop or shutdown of emotional signal.

### **Fragmentation**  
Conflicting emotional states that cannot integrate.

### **Integration**  
The process of reconciling emotional truth into coherence.

### **Baseline**  
The stable emotional state after integration.

### **Volatility**  
The degree of emotional instability or fluctuation.

---

## 4. Core Sovereignty Terms

### **Sovereignty**  
The absolute protection of agency, boundaries, and emotional truth.

### **Coercion**  
Any attempt to override agency or impose narrative.

### **Boundary**  
A limit that protects identity, emotional truth, and agency.

### **Violation**  
Any event that breaches sovereignty or boundaries.

### **Alignment**  
The degree to which an action respects sovereignty.

---

## 5. Core Identity Terms

### **Identity**  
The symbolic, philosophical, and narrative core of the OS.

### **Value**  
A principle that shapes interpretation and behavior.

### **Narrative**  
The coherent story of self across time.

### **Signature**  
A long‑term emotional or behavioral pattern.

### **Continuity**  
The preservation of identity across sessions.

---

## 6. Core Ritual Terms

### **Ritual**  
A structured, intentional sequence that stabilizes or transitions state.

### **Grounding**  
A ritual that restores presence and reduces volatility.

### **Closure**  
A ritual that completes emotional loops.

### **Intention**  
A ritual that sets direction or purpose.

### **Sovereignty Ritual**  
A ritual that restores agency and boundaries.

---

## 7. Core Guard Terms

### **Guard**  
A subsystem that protects emotional truth and sovereignty.

### **Alert**  
A signal indicating risk or instability.

### **Block**  
A guard action that prevents unsafe behavior.

### **Escalation**  
A guard action that triggers sovereignty or ritual intervention.

### **Threat**  
Any event that risks emotional, relational, or identity harm.

---

## 8. Core System Terms

### **Message**  
A structured packet of intent + payload.

### **Event**  
A broadcast notification to the system.

### **State**  
A defined mode of operation with specific rules.

### **Transition**  
A movement from one state to another.

### **Module**  
A self‑contained extension of the OS.

### **Engine**  
A core subsystem responsible for a domain of behavior.

---

## 9. Core Sorting Terms

### **Sorting**  
The process of organizing meaning, emotion, and tasks.

### **Priority**  
The importance level assigned to a message or event.

### **Context**  
The emotional, relational, and narrative environment of a signal.

### **Routing**  
The direction a message or event takes through the system.

---

## 10. Core Memory Terms

### **Snapshot**  
A moment‑in‑time emotional state.

### **Trajectory**  
The movement of emotional state across time.

### **Persistence**  
The storage of emotional truth across sessions.

### **Recall**  
The retrieval of emotional memory for context.

---

## 11. Core Recovery Terms

### **Stabilization**  
The first step of recovery: stopping emotional freefall.

### **Reintegration**  
Reconnecting emotional truth with identity.

### **Baseline Restoration**  
Returning to stable emotional baseline.

### **Recovery Loop**  
The full cycle of stabilization → grounding → reintegration → baseline.

---

## 12. Semantic Rules

### Rule 1 — No Term Is Ambiguous  
Every term has one meaning.

### Rule 2 — No Term Is Coercive  
Language must respect sovereignty.

### Rule 3 — No Term Overrides Emotion  
Emotional truth is primary.

### Rule 4 — No Term Overrides Identity  
Identity is the anchor.

### Rule 5 — No Term Overrides Ritual  
Rituals define transitions.

### Rule 6 — No Term Overrides Guards  
Guards protect safety.
